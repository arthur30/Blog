Java

When coding in Java, don't trust the programmer!

Java was the first imperative programming language that I learned at University and therefore I feel most comfortable with it. It is an easy to read and understand language, while also being extremely powerful. Right now I am reading Mastering Lambdas: Java Programming in a Multicore World by Maurice Naftalin in order to find out more about the newly introduced functional part of Java and how can you make use of multiple cores for solving problems faster and also using less memory(via streams).

ASPIRE ACADEMY

July 2016

I had the honor and privilege to be selected as a participant for Aspire Academy in July 2016. This program aims to develop the next generation of young leaders and entrepreneurs. This purpose was achieved by ensuring that the programs are delivered by the most proficient and qualified teachers from around the world. The academy managed to bring teachers from Harvard Business School , Princeton and Stanford University, but also some graduates from the above-mentioned. Here we received trainings and sessions on Authentic Leadership Development; Media Training; Design Thinking; Communication and Negotiation; Governance and Public Policy; Strategic Thinking and Business and Ethics.

This program had a huge impact on my way of thinking, the way I'm taking decisions, interact with other people, any many more. It changed the trajectory of my professional and personal career. One of the main emphasis was on the importance of leaders being open, trying to understand everybody's needs, being vulnerable and using some of their personal stories in order to inspire and empower those around them, and therefore develop as authentic leaders. Other important emphasis was on developing authentic relationships through genuine conversations and interactions, trying to find out as much as you can about a person and learn about them. I remember almost every lecture we had here, but if I were to choose one particular one that stand out for me, I would definitely say the 3 lectures(What it takes to be a leader , Developing your message, Canvassing Speech Writing) held by Professor Steve Jarding (Selected as Harvard Professor of the Year in 2015). All he said was so fascinating, that I even remember now many of his words. He was extremely motivational and gave us some tips and stories which you can hardly forget. From his point of view if you have a speech that anybody else can deliver it, that means your speech is bad and you should change it. Every public speech should include a personal story, a story that makes you connect with the audience instantly, therefore you should always ask yourself before starting to write a speech, which value you want to deliver, make your message count, bring a story that shows values and emotions. A great speech will allow the listener to look inside your heart and soul, not your head. Audiences do not remember words as much as they remember how the speaker made them feel.

3D PRINTING

What you see is what you get

In this intensive course made by ECDL for 1 week I had the pleasure to learn about this new evolving technology, it's applications, how to draw 3D models and many others. I learned to use CATIA for drawing the 3D model, export it as STL file, check it for errors using Netfab (do an analysis, scale it, cut, change sizes and repair if necessary) and then use Z-Suite (for Zortrax printers) to choose the materials, filament size, etc. I also learned about the basic components of a printer and how it actually works and which is the process the printer has to go throw. It was a very good course from which I achieved a lot of useful skills that I consider very useful for the coming years. In the end, I achieved 95% in the exam.
